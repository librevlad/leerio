Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 24, 2026

Overview

Leerio is an audiobook player that prioritizes your privacy. The app is designed to work offline-first — your audiobook files and listening progress are stored locally on your device by default.

Data We Collect

Without an account (free local player):

  • No personal data is collected
  • Your audiobook files stay on your device
  • Listening progress is stored in your browser's local storage and IndexedDB
  • No data is sent to our servers

With an account (optional):

  • Email address — used for authentication only
  • Listening progress — synced to our server so you can continue on another device
  • Uploaded audiobook files — stored on our servers (Vultr Object Storage, Amsterdam) for cloud access
  • Library metadata — book titles, authors, bookmarks, notes, collections

Anonymous usage analytics:

  • We collect anonymous, aggregated usage events (e.g., "play button clicked", "book opened") to improve the app
  • No personally identifiable information is included
  • No third-party analytics services are used

How We Store Data

  • Local data: IndexedDB and localStorage in your browser/app — never leaves your device unless you enable cloud sync
  • Server data: SQLite database and Vultr S3-compatible object storage in Amsterdam, Netherlands
  • Audio files: Stored as-is, not processed or analyzed

Third-Party Services

  • Google OAuth — if you choose to sign in with Google. We receive only your name and email. Google Privacy Policy
  • Vultr Object Storage — for cloud audiobook storage. Vultr Privacy Policy

Your Rights

  • You can use Leerio without creating an account — no data leaves your device
  • You can delete your account and all associated data at any time via Settings
  • You can export your data by contacting us

Device Permissions (Android)

  • Read audio files — to scan your audiobook folders and build your library
  • Internet — for cloud sync and the book catalog (optional)

Children's Privacy

Leerio is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

We may update this policy. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
